At the entrance to the city park Valkenberg, a monument that tells you something about the wordings history of the city of Breda, but especially about the men of the city of the House of Nassau and the 500-year-old bond between Breda and house of Nassau.
On July 3, 1905 the Nassau-Baronie monument, as it is officially called, with lots of festive parade unveiled by Queen Wilhelmina. The image recalls the fact that in 1404 Count Engelbert, the first Breda Nassau and his wife, Johanna van Polans were inaugurated as Lord and Lady of Breda.
The designer is the famous Dr. P.J.H. Cuypers, who designed i.a. the Rijksmuseum and Amsterdam Central Station.
On this monument is not only the arms of twenty municipalities in and around the Barony applied but also the Nassau Lion dominates everything with royal crown, sword and shield. In the three reliefs is the "joyful incomste 'Earl Engelbert and his wife displayed.
The Burgesses have the key to the city.
